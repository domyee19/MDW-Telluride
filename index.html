The Story

Four days through Colorado's Western Slope. One night of dispersed camping under high-desert stars, two nights on a private ranch with mountain views. Black Canyon of the Gunnison in the morning, Telluride and a film festival in the afternoon, and a high-mountain hike in between.

⚡ Mountainfilm Festival
Runs in Telluride May 21–25. Town will be busy and lodging tight, but Sunday afternoon screenings are the highlight of your weekend.

Day One · Friday May 22

Hartman Rocks

Denver → Gunnison · BLM dispersed · 7,700 ft

The Drive Out

~4 hours · 200 mi · US-285 → US-50 over Monarch Pass

The opening stretch — flexible departure once everyone's off work. Drive is straightforward and scenic: US-285 south through South Park, US-50 west over Monarch Pass (11,312 ft), drop into the Gunnison Valley. Hartman is 3 mi south of town. Aim to arrive at camp before dark (sunset ~8:25pm).

  • Depart Leave DenverTop off gas, grab car snacks · ~4 hr total
  • +2 hr SalidaGas + bathroom break; last real services before Monarch Pass
  • +3 hr Monarch Pass summitStop for a photo if you want · 11,312 ft
  • +3:45 GunnisonFill water at Jorgensen Park, optional dinner pickup
  • +4 hr Arrive Hartman RocksFind a site, set up before dark
⏱ Departure planning
Aim to leave Denver by ~4 PM to reach camp in daylight. Later than 5 PM means setting up tents in the dark — bring headlamps to the top of the gear pile and consider eating dinner before you leave.
Monarch Pass · Sawatch Range · 11,312 ft · Wikimedia Commons

Hartman Rocks Base Area

BLM · 14,000 acres · 50 dispersed sites · Free · FCFS

High-desert BLM land south of Gunnison. Sagebrush and slickrock at 7,700 ft, much warmer at night than the alpine campgrounds. Network of dispersed sites along well-maintained county and BLM roads. Year-round, no reservations, no fees.

Hartman Rocks Recreation Area · BLM Colorado / Wikimedia Commons

Stop 1

Black Canyon of the Gunnison

South Rim · National Park · $30/vehicle

One of the most underrated national parks. The Painted Wall is the tallest sheer cliff in Colorado at 2,250 ft — taller than the Empire State Building. Plan for 3 hours: visitor center, drive the overlooks, do Warner Point as the main hike.

Painted Wall · 2,250 ft sheer cliff · Photo: Jesse Varner / Wikimedia Commons

Warner Point Nature Trail

1.5 mi RT · moderate · ~1 hr · ~8,300 ft elevation. Trailhead at High Point, end of South Rim Road. Pinyon-juniper forest, shady benches, opens to panoramic views: San Juan Range to the south, West Elk Mountains to the north, deepest part of Black Canyon below. Pick up the trail guide at the trailhead or visitor center.

Overlooks to hit on the drive

  • Tomichi Point · first one, easy access
  • Pulpit Rock · short out-and-back, dramatic perched view
  • Cedar Point · 0.2 mi walk to Painted Wall views
  • Dragon Point · alternate Painted Wall angle
  • Sunset View · western end of the road
⚠ 2026 Closure
South Rim Campground closed due to fire damage from the 2025 South Rim Fire. Day-use only.

Stop 3

The Hastings View

Hartwork Ranch · Hipcamp · ~8,500 ft · reserved
The Hastings View · Hartwork Ranch · Hipcamp listing

40-acre ranch on Hastings Mesa with views of Hayden's Peak and Alder Canyon. May is wildflower peak — iris and clematis through the meadows. Lower elevation than the alpine alternatives, so warmer nights. Site is the highest on the property, separate from others, with the best peak views.

Morning

Jud Wiebe Trail

3 mi loop · moderate · 1,200 ft gain · ~2 hrs
Bridal Veil Falls · 365 ft · tallest free-falling waterfall in Colorado

The classic in-town hike. Loops up the north side of the box canyon from Aspen Street trailhead, climbs to a saddle with a spectacular overlook of Telluride and the surrounding peaks, and descends back. No backtracking. Perfect for the morning before festival time.

Afternoon

Mountainfilm Festival

Sunday programming · single tickets at venues
Colorado Avenue during festival week · Wikimedia Commons

Annual documentary and adventure film festival, runs Memorial Day weekend in Telluride. Sunday is the festival's third day — full programming, strong audience energy, filmmakers around for Q&As. You don't need a full pass; just buy single program tickets at the door.

How it works

  • Single tickets · $20 each at venue · credit card only (no cash)
  • Programs run ~90 min · usually 4-6 short films grouped thematically
  • Pick by topic, not time slot — themes range from climbing to climate to social justice
  • Standby line for non-passholders · show up 30+ min early to get in
  • Free outdoor shorts play in Elks Park (downtown) — drop in anytime
